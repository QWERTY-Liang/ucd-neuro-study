What you'll do

You watch simple shapes on a screen and show your choice by pressing a button, moving your eyes, or making a small hand movement, in short 10-minute rounds with breaks. We put a soft cap on your head to record brain signals (a gel that washes out with water) and stick small sensors on your hands, cheek and forehead. You can do it in one longer visit (about 5 hours) or split it over two shorter days (about 3.5 hours each) — you choose when you book. It takes place in the lab (Room 022, engineering building, UCD).

Who can take part

You can take part ifYou're aged 18–50, can see well (glasses/contacts are fine), have no brain or mental-health condition that slows fast reactions, and can roll up your sleeves for arm sensors.
Please don't if you haveEpilepsy, fainting for no clear reason, trouble with flashing lights, a silver allergy, or any implanted electronic device (e.g. a pacemaker).

Your data & what happens on the day

We store your data under a code, not your name, and follow data-protection law (GDPR). Taking part is your choice and you can stop at any time. When you arrive we'll go through and sign the consent form together and do a quick handedness check — nothing to fill in beforehand. Showers, shampoo and towels are there for you afterwards.

About the lab & our research

We're the Cognitive Neural Systems Lab at University College Dublin, led by Dr Elaine Corbett. We study how the brain turns what we see into decisions — and how those decisions are shaped by the movements we make to act on them.

To do this, we record the brain's electrical signals (EEG) and small muscle signals (EMG) while people do simple decision tasks. Learning how a healthy brain gathers evidence and commits to a choice can, in time, help build better tools for conditions that affect decision-making and movement. The work is funded by Science Foundation Ireland.
